Frequently Asked Questions

Q. How do auditions work?

A. Our audition process comprises a round of preliminary auditions with tests of vocal range, tonal memory, sight singing, and solo ability, and a second round of callbacks. Preliminary auditions last 15-20 minutes. You will be notified a few hours to a few days after your preliminary audition about whether you have made it through to callbacks. Callbacks can take up to the entire day depending upon how many auditionees are called back. However, we do make accommodations for those with time constraints.

Q. What do I need to do to prepare for a preliminary audition?

A. We strongly recommend that auditionees warmup prior to the preliminary audition, as we will be testing your upper and lower range, and ability to follow our warmup exercise. We do assume most of our auditionees to be able to sight read music so it might be worthwhile to brush up on that a little bit. We also expect auditionees to prepare a solo piece between 1-2 minutes long – about the length of a verse and chorus of a pop song.

Q. But I can’t sight read! What should I do?

A. Some sight-reading ability is expected. However, sight-singing remains very difficult for most musicians, and we do not expect perfection. In the audition, we look for ability follow rhythm and basic intervals. Additonally, we have tonal memory exercises to see how well members can learn music by ear. The purpose of these tests is to see how quickly you pick up new music, as we do expect members to learn music fast.

Q. What should I sing for my audition?

A. While there are no strict guidelines on what to sing for your solo, we ask that you prepare a piece in English, in a genre that we perform. Pop, rock, jazz, R&B, Broadway songs are good choices. Practice singing your solo a cappella, in a key you’re comfortable with.

Q. But I’m nervous!

A. We know. We understand that the audition process can be a nerve-wracking one. We’ve been there and were probably nervous trainwrecks at our own auditions, so we understand. We’ll try to get you to relax so you can perform to the best of your abilities.

Q. How do callbacks work?

A. If you are given a callback, congratulations! You will be given 1-2 short pieces to learn. The purpose of this is to see how well your voice is able to blend with the current group. You will also be asked to perform your solo piece again, along with some of the exercises from the preliminary audition individually in front of all of the members of the current group.

Q. How is DtS different from all the other groups?

A. Drawn to Scale coalesced in Fall of 2008, so we are still fairly young and figuring ourselves out. As a result, we are constantly striving to make ourselves known, through creating innovative arrangements and finding new ways to beome more involved in the Cal community. Being part of such a new group requires a lot of hard work and dedication, but can also be very rewarding as we establish precedents that can be followed by Drawn to Scale for years to come. We also consider ourselves a very social a cappella group, and are always planning dinners, parties, and retreats in order to facilitate group bonding and have fun. Our hope is that DtS can be not just a place to make great music, but also a place to make great friends.

Q. What are the time commitments involved?

A. Our rehearsals are Monday and Wednesdays 7-9:30 pm, apart from weekly one-hour sectionals. We also have weekly Sproul performances on Friday at noon, with warmups beginning at 11.15 am. We also have other events during the semester as well as a concert at the end of each semester. We expect members to be able to make all rehearsals and Sproul performances, and a number of other events. Being a member of an a cappella group could be a significant time commitment, but we enjoy every moment spent together as a performance group. All our members can tell you that they have a lot of fun performing and making memories with the group.

Q. Should I audition again if I have auditioned previously?

A. Definitely! Selections at the preliminary and the callback levels are always very difficult for us due to our limited time and the number of voices per part we need changes from semester to semester. We have seen many wonderful singers who showed great promise and simply needed a little bit more of experience in one area or another. Especially if we asked you to work on something in your previous audition, we would love to see you again. Some of our members have also auditioned multiple times before getting into the group, so don’t be afraid to re-audition.
